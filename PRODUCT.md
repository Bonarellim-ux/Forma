# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Beginner-to-intermediate lifters who train on a recurring weekly split (push/pull/legs, upper/lower, etc.). They open the app at the gym, mid-session, often one-handed and between sets, and again at home to review progress. Their core job: know what to do today and what weight/reps to aim for, with a reason they can trust, then log it fast.

## Product Purpose

Forma turns workout logs into explainable, evidence-based training recommendations. It exists because most fitness apps either guess confidently or bury the user in numbers. Forma's differentiator: **no recommendation is better than a weak recommendation**, and every recommendation must be explainable. Success = the user trusts the suggestion, logs the session in seconds, and sees honest progress over weeks.

## Brand Personality

Bold and athletic, but earned, not hyped. Confident like a strong coach who shows their reasoning rather than shouting motivation. Three words: **confident, precise, athletic.** The interface should feel like a serious training instrument, performance-driven and decisive, while staying calm and legible at the moment of use (mid-set, tired, in a hurry).

## Anti-references

- **Hype / bro fitness apps:** loud rainbow gradients, "transform your body" claims, fake urgency, hustle-culture copy. Forma earns confidence through reasoning, not volume.
- **Cluttered tracker apps (MyFitnessPal-style):** dense grids of undifferentiated numbers, no hierarchy, everything the same weight. Forma shows one clear next action.
- **Gamified / cartoonish apps:** mascots, confetti, playful illustrations, badge spam that undercut credibility.
- (Cross-register) Generic AI-SaaS: purple gradients, decorative glassmorphism, hero-metric templates, identical card grids.

## Design Principles

1. **One clear next action.** Every screen answers "what do I do now?" before it shows anything secondary. The day's workout and its start action own the top of the home screen.
2. **Show the reasoning.** Recommendations carry a visible, plain-language "why." Confidence comes from transparency, not assertion.
3. **Built for the gym moment.** Legible at arm's length, tappable one-handed, fast under fatigue. Touch targets, contrast, and weight hierarchy serve a distracted user mid-set.
4. **Honest restraint.** When there isn't a strong recommendation, say so plainly. No filler, no manufactured streaks-pressure, no number for its own sake.
5. **Athletic confidence in form.** Decisive type, strong contrast, committed accent color, purposeful motion. Bold without being loud.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, large/bold text ≥3:1 (the current light-gray-on-tint muted text is a known risk to fix). Touch targets ≥44px for in-gym one-handed use. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Support light environments (bright gym lighting) and the existing kg/lb unit toggle. Don't rely on color alone to distinguish split types (pair with labels).
